Case 01

Local lender package

1-page ROI sheet

Headline: Run more private loans without adding operational headcount.

  • Cost of manual work: active loans x minutes saved x hourly ops cost.
  • Primary proof: fewer setup steps, fewer missed borrower tasks, cleaner payment tracking.
  • CTA: compare one month of manual ops time to one month of Baseline.

Local lender case study frame

Before: loan files lived across email, spreadsheets, shared folders, and manual ACH notes.

After: one pipeline, borrower portal, servicing record, and payment history.

Metric story: loan setup time down, borrower follow-up cycles down, loan capacity up.

Spreadsheet replacement checklist

  • Do loan balances require manual checking?
  • Are borrower documents tracked in email threads?
  • Can the team instantly see every active loan status?
  • Are payoff statements created manually?
  • Is investor or borrower communication easy to audit?

Regional demo script

  1. Open standardized loan product templates.

Workflow standardization map

Current state: each loan officer manages process differently.

Baseline state: templates, stages, task owners, borrower portal, servicing history, and reporting all share one operating model.

30/60/90 implementation plan

  • 30 days: migrate pipeline, configure templates, onboard first users.
  • 60 days: standardize draw workflows and servicing reports.
  • 90 days: launch management dashboards and investor-facing reporting cadence.

Case 03

National lender package

Enterprise security brief

  • Role-based access by team, function, and seniority.
  • Audit trails for loan changes, payment events, and communication history.
  • Centralized borrower, investor, servicing, and capital data.

Capital operations deep dive

Position Baseline as the control layer for funds, whole-note investors, fractional participation, automated distributions, and investor reporting.

Executive business case

Thesis: high-volume private lenders do not just need more origination tools; they need one auditable system of record across loans, payments, capital, and reporting.

Decision metric: reduced reporting prep, lower operational risk, faster servicing review, and stronger investor confidence.
